Danielle Gruen, PhD

Scientist & Medical Student, University of Pittsburgh

Research

My research is focused on understanding biological responses to trauma and prehospital interventions. In other words, who gets sick the fastest following traumatic injury, and why? How can we quickly identify these patients and adapt their care in order to improve outcomes?

I leverage ground and helicopter emergency medical services (EMS) information, clinical data, and biological time series measurements to understand how prehospital interventions may affect the biology of injured patients. My work is mentored by investigators from the Department of Surgery, Department of Emergency Medicine, and Pittsburgh Trauma Research Center.

Training

I received a PhD in Biogeochemistry from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution. Using experimental and computational approaches, I investigated stable isotope and phylogenetic signals diagnostic of microbial metabolisms. I also completed the Harvard-MIT Health Sciences and Technology Graduate Education in Medical Sciences certificate program.

I applied this training to address clinical challenges as an NIH T32 Postdoctoral Fellow in Trauma and Sepsis in the Billiar Lab.

Interests

I am interested in urban and wilderness EMS, and have worked for and learned from organizations including Foxwall, MIT EMS, NOLS WMI, and Penn State UAS. I spend my free time adventuring in the outdoors as a climber, runner, and member of The Explorers Club.
