About Danielle

I am a PhD candidate in Biogeochemistry jointly with the MIT Department of Earth, Atmospheric and Planetary Sciences and the WHOI Department of Marine Chemistry and Geochemistry. I am also part of the Harvard-MIT Health Sciences & Technology Graduate Education in Medical Sciences Training Program. 

Broadly, I aim to characterize the origin, evolution, and interactions of life and Earth. My thesis research explores diagnostic signals of microbial metabolisms over geologic time scales and in the modern environment, both theoretically and experimentally, by borrowing tools from fields such as geochemistry, microbiology, and bioinformatics. My research is interdisciplinary, motivated by unanswered questions in geobiology and dedicated to improving the human condition. In my spare time I like to climb mountains and volunteer as an EMT and in outreach for the MIT Ambulance.
