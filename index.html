About Danielle

I am a PhD candidate at MIT and WHOI in Geobiology and Chemical Oceanography. I am also part of the Harvard-MIT Health Sciences & Technology Graduate Education in Medical Sciences (GEMS) Training Program. Broadly, I aim to characterize the origin, evolution, and interactions of life and Earth. I explore life and Earth processes over long time scales and in the modern environment, both theoretically and experimentally, by borrowing tools from fields such as geochemistry, microbiology, and bioinformatics. My research interests are interdisciplinary, motivated by unanswered questions in astrobiology and dedicated to improving the human condition. In my spare time I like to climb mountains and volunteer as an EMT and in outreach for the MIT Ambulance (@MIT_EMS).
